📖 How-To Guide

Writing Better Prompts

⏱️ 10–15 minutes 🟢 Beginner friendly 🤖 Works with any AI tool

The quality of the response you get from an AI tool like Claude, ChatGPT, or Copilot is almost entirely determined by the quality of your prompt. A vague question gets a vague answer. A well-structured prompt gets something genuinely useful. This guide walks you through the key principles with real before-and-after examples.

🎯

What you'll learn: How to structure prompts, give the right context, set the tone, use examples effectively, and avoid the most common mistakes.

🔬 Anatomy of a great prompt

Most great prompts share the same building blocks. You don't need all of them every time, but understanding each one helps you know what to include when.

Role You are an experienced IT trainer writing for a non-technical audience.
Context I'm creating a beginner guide for colleagues who have never used GitHub before.
Task Write a step-by-step guide for setting up GitHub Pages.
Format Use numbered steps, keep each step to 2–3 sentences, and include a tip box at the end.
Example Here's an example of the tone I want: "Click the green button in the top right — it's the one labelled New Repository."
💡

You don't need all five parts every time. For a quick question, just the task might be enough. For complex or high-stakes outputs, add as many parts as you can.

🎯 Principle 1 — Be specific, not vague

The single biggest improvement most people can make is simply being more specific. AI tools are very good at following precise instructions, but they'll make assumptions when you leave things unclear.

1

Say exactly what you want

Include the topic, the purpose, the audience, and any constraints. The more specific you are, the less guessing the AI has to do.

❌ Vague

"Write something about Azure."

✅ Specific

"Write a 200-word plain-English summary of what Azure is, aimed at someone who has never used cloud computing before."

2

Specify the length and format

If you don't say how long or what format you want, the AI will guess. Tell it upfront.

❌ No format

"Explain how Intune works."

✅ With format

"Explain how Intune works in 5 bullet points, each one a single sentence, suitable for a team briefing."

🗂️ Principle 2 — Give context

Context helps the AI tailor its response to your actual situation. Think of it like briefing a colleague — the more background you give them, the better their output will be.

3

Explain who the output is for

The same question gets very different answers depending on the audience. Always mention who will be reading or using the output.

❌ No audience

"Explain what a DNS record is."

✅ With audience

"Explain what a DNS record is to someone who works in HR and has no IT background. Avoid jargon and use a simple analogy."

4

Share relevant background information

If you're working on something specific — a project, a document, a system — tell the AI about it. You can even paste in existing text for it to work with.

❌ No background

"Write an email about the system outage."

✅ With background

"Write a brief email to end users explaining that our M365 email service was down from 9am–11am today due to a Microsoft outage, and that all services have now been restored. Keep it under 100 words and professional in tone."

🎭 Principle 3 — Assign a role

Telling the AI to act as a specific type of expert dramatically improves the quality and relevance of the response. It sets the right tone, vocabulary, and level of depth.

5

Use "You are a..." to set the persona

Start your prompt by assigning a role. This works especially well for writing tasks, technical explanations, and training content.

❌ No role

"Create a quiz about Azure fundamentals."

✅ With role

"You are an experienced Microsoft certified trainer. Create a 5-question multiple choice quiz testing basic Azure fundamentals. Include the correct answers at the end."

Goal Useful role to assign
Writing training contentExperienced IT trainer writing for beginners
Simplifying technical docsTechnical writer specialising in plain English
Reviewing a documentSenior editor with an eye for clarity and structure
Solving a technical problemSenior cloud engineer with 10 years of Azure experience
Writing a professional emailSenior IT manager communicating with stakeholders

📋 Principle 4 — Use examples

Showing the AI an example of what you want — even a rough one — is one of the most powerful techniques available. It removes ambiguity instantly.

6

Show, don't just tell

If you have an existing document, email, or style you want the AI to match, paste it in and say "write something in this style." This works better than trying to describe the style in words.

❌ No example

"Write a brief, friendly, informal guide step."

✅ With example

"Write a guide step in this style: 'Head to Settings — it's the gear icon on the left sidebar. Don't worry if yours looks slightly different; the layout can vary depending on your plan.'"

🔁 Principle 5 — Iterate and refine

You don't have to get the perfect prompt first time. The best results often come from a short back-and-forth conversation where you build on the AI's first response.

7

Use follow-up prompts to refine

If the first response isn't quite right, don't start over. Tell the AI what to change. Be specific about what you liked and what needs adjusting.

  • → "That's good but make it shorter — aim for half the length."
  • → "Rewrite the second paragraph in a less formal tone."
  • → "Add a practical example to point number 3."
  • → "Can you turn this into a table instead?"
  • → "Great — now adapt this for a technical audience."
💡

Think of prompting like briefing a very capable assistant. You wouldn't expect a colleague to get a complex piece of work perfect from a one-line instruction — and neither should you expect that from AI.

⚠️ Common mistakes to avoid

!

Being too polite or too casual

You don't need to say "please" and "thank you" (it won't make the AI work harder) but equally, don't be so brief that you strip out important detail. Aim for clear and direct.

!

Asking too many things at once

If you need five different things, consider sending five separate prompts. Combining too many tasks in one go often results in the AI doing all of them poorly rather than any of them well.

!

Accepting the first response without reviewing it

AI can produce confident-sounding answers that contain errors or miss the point. Always read the response critically, fact-check anything important, and don't be afraid to push back.

⚠️

Important: AI tools can occasionally get facts wrong or make things up. Always verify important information from a trusted source before using it in official documents or communications.

⚡ Quick reference — prompt starters

Copy and adapt these prompt templates to get started quickly:

TaskPrompt starter template
Explain a concept Explain [topic] in plain English for someone who [audience description]. Keep it under [length].
Write a guide You are an IT trainer. Write a step-by-step guide for [task]. Audience: [description]. Format: numbered steps.
Summarise a document Summarise the key points from the following text in [X] bullet points. Focus on [specific aspect]: [paste text]
Write an email Write a [formal/informal] email to [audience] about [topic]. Keep it under [length]. Key points to include: [list]
Create a quiz Create a [X]-question multiple choice quiz on [topic] for [audience]. Include answers at the end.
Review/improve writing Review the following text for clarity and tone. Suggest improvements but keep the overall structure: [paste text]
